Milk choclate

Milk chocolate is known by several names, including: mixed dairy chocolate, or skimmed milk chocolate, milk chocolate is made from several ingredients, which are as follows

Raw choclate

Raw Chocolate from other types of chocolate is that when prepared, milk is never heated to more than 45° to keep antioxidants, nutrients, and enzymes in good condition, unlike other chocolates that are heated until they reach a temperature of 130°

Ruby choclate

Ruby Chocolate is defined as one of the types of chocolate discovered in 2009, which is made from chocolate beans produced from the cocoa tree, but these grains are naturally pink, that is, without adding any flavors or colors, and contain a special mixture of compounds that are likely to be polyphenols that give them a distinctive color

White choclate

White Chocolate is not actually a type of chocolate, because it does not contain chocolate solids, but it consists of cocoa butter, sugar, milk products, vanilla extract, and a fatty substance known as lecithin.
